System Interaction Model

How the five frameworks depend on, govern, and reinforce one another — the architecture of Compassionism as a unified system.

Each framework occupies a distinct functional layer. Understanding their relationships is essential for implementation sequencing — dependencies must be in place before dependent systems can operate effectively.

Economic Layer
CCO
Creative Currency Octaves — generates value signals and circulation incentives through the dual-currency system
Asset Layer
PTF
Public Trust Foundations — builds collective ownership and community wealth through community-operated businesses
Asset Layer
PTH
Public Trust Housing — creates collective housing equity through resident-owned community properties
Spatial Layer
SZH
Social Zone Harmonization — organizes physical and social structure through coordinated community planning
Democratic Layer
CIP
Citizens Internet Portal — governs currency operations and democratic participation across all systems

Framework Dependencies & Relationships

Relationship Description
PTF depends_on CCO PTF accepts basic units as payment; CCO funds PTF operations and provides the currency infrastructure PTF businesses require
PTH depends_on CCO Housing costs settled in basic units; CCO provides the economic security that makes collective housing participation viable
CIP governs CCO CIP manages conversion rates, merit assessment, and basic unit distribution — the democratic layer controls the economic engine
CIP governs PTF + PTH Democratic oversight of all collective assets; residents and members vote on key decisions through the CIP platform
SZH stabilizes PTF + PTH Zoning shapes service and housing deployment; SZH ensures PTF businesses and PTH communities are spatially coherent
CCO feeds_into SZH Creative collectives anchor zone formation; economic activity patterns inform how social zones are structured and evolved
PTF enhances CCO PTF venues reduce Creative Collective overhead by 40–60%; PTF workers receive a 50% conversion bonus, strengthening the CCO incentive structure
