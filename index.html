About The Research Initiative

This initiative presents 14 peer-reviewable papers focused on eliminating poverty through dual-currency systems and community ownership models.

Core Mission: Develop systems that guarantee basic needs while rewarding productivity and community contribution.

Key Frameworks

The following interconnected architectures form a comprehensive system for rational public affairs.

Creative Currency Octaves (CCO)
A dual-currency system where citizens opt-in for 'basic units' (BU) for essentials that expire monthly.
  • System: Dual-Currency
  • Mechanism: 1x-9x Multiplier
Public Trust Housing (PTH)
Community-owned housing held in public trust, allowing for collective wealth building and resident control.
Public Trust Foundations (PTF)
Not-for-profit community businesses (grocers, utilities) operated for collective benefit.
Citizens Internet Portal (CIP)
Digital infrastructure enabling secure democratic participation and CCO currency management.
