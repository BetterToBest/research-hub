CCO-PTF-PTH-CIP-SZH Simulation Replication Framework

Complete Technical Package — BLEI Temporal Stability Integration and Extraction-Accurate Agent Modeling
SYSTEM

Compassionism

The overarching framework developed by Duke Johnson, integrating five coordinated economic and governance architectures into a single system designed to structurally eliminate poverty while preserving market incentives and democratic governance.

CCO

Creative Currency Octaves

A dual-currency economic layer. Citizens opt in to receive Basic Units (BU) — a monthly allocation (~$1,200/adult) redeemable at PTF businesses for essential goods. Businesses that accept BU convert them to primary currency at merit-scaled rates (1×–9×), rewarding quality and community contribution.

BU

Basic Units

The community currency issued by the CCO system. Each BU is pegged 1:1 to the primary currency but redeemable at PTF establishments at below-market prices — generating a purchasing power premium (ε) of 2.64× for food and 1.80× for utilities. BU expire monthly, ensuring they circulate toward genuine need.

PTF

Public Trust Foundations

Community-owned, democratically governed businesses — grocers, counter-service restaurants, utilities, childcare — that accept Basic Units and operate on a not-for-profit model. By eliminating shareholder profit extraction, PTF establishments achieve 40–60% overhead reductions (analogous to Mondragon's cooperative model), enabling the below-market pricing that generates the BU purchasing power premium.

PTH

Public Trust Housing

Community-owned residential housing in which monthly payments ($600/month at base case) accrue entirely to the resident's Acre Equity account rather than being lost to market rent or mortgage interest. This transforms a traditionally extractive expense into a wealth-building mechanism, analogous to how Community Land Trusts operate in practice across 46 US states.

AE

Acre Equity

The cumulative community equity built by PTH residents through their monthly payments, appreciating at approximately 4% per year. Unlike conventional home equity, Acre Equity is accessible at exit with a liquidity haircut that decreases as tenure increases — from ~10–20% in the first six months to ~80–90% at five or more years.

SZH

Social Zone Harmonization

The spatial governance layer that organizes the geographic deployment of PTF businesses and PTH communities within designated zones. SZH governs network density thresholds — the minimum PTF merchant participation needed to activate cooperative synergy effects (θ), which require at least 55% zone participation before activating.

CIP

Citizens Internet Portal

A privacy-first digital governance platform that administers CCO currency operations — conversion, distribution, merit assessment — and manages democratic voting for PTH and PTF decisions. CIP provides the transparent institutional layer that prevents elite capture and validates the system's democratic legitimacy.

BLEI

Basic Living Economic Index

The primary welfare metric in this framework. BLEI expresses how many days of basic living a household's accessible resources cover — a temporal stability measure. The BLEI numerator uses food BU effective value ($990/adult/month) because food is excluded from C_basic; utility BU offset the cash utility cost already in C_basic (net zero BLEI contribution). Full BU purchasing power ($2,502) is captured in the EPPM index. See the BLEI Foundation Paper for the complete accounting convention.

EDC

Extractive Drain Coefficient

The fraction of gross income captured by financial contracts that build zero household wealth — rent, mortgage interest, consumer debt interest, predatory fees. The EDC is the measurement that reveals why two households earning $2,083/month can experience radically different economic trajectories: one paying extractive rent (EDC ~0.625); one building Acre Equity (EDC ~0.025).

FBS

Financial Bandwidth Score

Monthly residual resources above the basic living threshold — the "slack" that allows households to invest in education, creative work, or business formation. Grounded in Mullainathan & Shafir's (2013) scarcity cognition research: without positive FBS, cognitive bandwidth is consumed by immediate survival, making advancement structurally impossible regardless of individual capability. FBS uses EDC_residual (consumer debt only) to avoid double-counting housing costs in C_basic.

EPPM

Effective Purchasing Power Multiplier

A dimensionless ratio expressing effective purchasing power of a CCO-PTF household relative to a baseline household at the same nominal income. EPPM uses full BU·ε_blended = $2,502 (food + utility BU value) because it measures total purchasing power advantage versus market provision. At the poverty line, EPPM = 5.80×.

CSI

Compound Stability Index

A forward-looking projection of BLEI trajectory over time, compounding initial stability by net wealth growth rate adjusted for extractive drain, plus accumulated Acre Equity. W_accessible (which initializes BLEI) excludes AE entirely; r_wealth and r_a evolve independently to prevent double-counting. All time indices in months; annual rates use monthly compounding.

IPBI

Indirect Participant Benefit Index

Estimated annual welfare gains accruing to high-wealth households who do not directly participate in CCO-PTF, estimated at $22,800–$59,900 per year (zero direct participation required). Channel weights use distinct notation (α, β, π, δ, ζ) to avoid symbol conflict with the BLEI liquidity coefficient γ.

These terms are formally defined and mathematically specified in the BLEI Foundation Paper, including Appendix A (Symbol Table) and Appendix B (Worked Numerical Example). The full Compassionism system architecture is documented in the Executive Summary & Research Index.

Methodology Overview

This replication framework operationalizes the BLEI welfare measurement suite as a computational agent-based simulation. It is a calibrated institutional simulation architecture — not a conventional econometric study — designed to identify optimal system parameters and test framework behavior across thousands of parameter draws. Key parameters (ε, r_a, BU allocation) are grounded in three deployed real-world analogues: the Alaska Permanent Fund Dividend (BU allocation without dependency effects), Community Land Trusts across 46 US states (Acre Equity accumulation and r_a calibration), and Mondragon Corporation (cooperative overhead reduction underlying ε ≥ 2.0×). The BU purchasing power accounting convention distinguishes food BU contributions to BLEI ($990/adult/month, food excluded from C_basic) from utility BU contributions (offset cash utility cost in C_basic; full value captured in EPPM).

Step 1 — BLEI-Initialized Agents

Agents initialized with BLEI, EDC, FBS, and Acre Equity state variables drawn from calibrated US wealth and housing distributions. PTH payments route to Acre Equity from initialization, at the appropriate liquidity haircut by tenure cohort. W_accessible excludes AE to preserve CSI orthogonality.

Step 2 — Extraction-Accurate Dynamics

Monthly wealth updates distinguish extractive outflows (reduce BLEI, no AE) from generative payments (build AE, improve BLEI trajectory). PTH and market rent are modeled asymmetrically. FBS uses EDC_residual (consumer debt only) to avoid double-counting housing costs already in C_basic. BLEI uses food BU effective value ($990); EPPM uses BU·ε_blended ($2,502).

Step 3 — FBS-Gated Advancement

Octave advancement probability is gated by Financial Bandwidth Score. P(advance) = 1 − exp(−λ × FBS). Agents at FBS = 0 cannot advance regardless of quality score. This reflects Mullainathan & Shafir's (2013) cognitive bandwidth research. γ transitions from 0.12 (entry) to 0.20 (CCO-established) as the guaranteed BU floor matures.

Step 4 — Network-Density Synergy

Synergy coefficient θ activates only above 55% PTF merchant participation within a SZH zone, scaling linearly to 0.25 at 90%+ density. Below this threshold, cooperative pricing synergies do not activate and ε reflects early-phase efficiency (1.5–2.0×) rather than mature cooperative scale (2.64×). The ε cost pass-through model is detailed in Section 3.3 of the BLEI Foundation Paper.

Step 5 — Full-Cohort Welfare

High-wealth non-participants tracked separately with IPBI benefit flows representing crime reduction, public health improvement, property value appreciation, and labor productivity gains. Conservative lower-bound IPBI ($22,800/yr) is the preferred metric. Channel weight notation: α·CR + β·PH + π·LP + δ·PV + ζ·ME, where π (LP weight) is distinct from the BLEI liquidity coefficient γ.

Step 6 — EDC-Adjusted Gini

Gini reported on extraction-adjusted net wealth: W_net = W_nominal − (EDC × Y × 12). This corrects the standard Gini's blindness to the direction of financial flows: two households with identical nominal wealth but different EDC levels have structurally different inequality positions. Nominal Gini retained as secondary reference only.

BLEI Temporal Stability Tier System

The six BLEI tiers replace binary poverty/non-poverty classification and gate agent advancement probability in the simulation. Tier assignment derives from BLEI score (days of basic living covered). The Flourishing tier threshold of 730 days — two years — reflects the capital commitment horizon required for entrepreneurial ventures to become viable without required break-even within the first year. Tier characterizations are grounded in Carroll's (1997) buffer-stock saving theory and Mullainathan & Shafir's (2013) cognitive bandwidth research.

Agent Architecture — Extraction-Accurate Model

The corrected agent class adds six state variables and revises the wealth update function to separate extractive and generative financial flows. The FBS calculation uses EDC_residual (consumer debt interest only) rather than the full EDC to avoid double-counting housing costs already captured in C_basic_cash_monthly. γ is initialized at 0.12 (entry) and transitions toward 0.20 (CCO-established) as the guaranteed BU floor matures. BU_food_eff ($990) is used for BLEI; BU·ε_blended ($2,502) is used for EPPM. Key corrections from prior simulation code are annotated inline.

Grounded in Carroll (1997): guaranteed BU floor expands accessible income fraction.""" if not self.cco_participant: return 0.12 if self.tenure_months < 3: return 0.12 # Month 0–2: entry; floor not yet established elif self.tenure_months < 6: t = (self.tenure_months - 3) / 3.0 return 0.12 + t * (0.20 - 0.12) # linear transition return 0.20 # Month 6+: CCO floor established def _ae_haircut(self) -> float: """ Acre Equity liquidity haircut by PTH tenure. Calibrated against Community Land Trust exit data (Davis & Demetrowitz 2003). """ m = self.tenure_months if m < 7: return 0.15 elif m < 25: return 0.40 elif m < 61: return 0.65 # base-case projection cohort else: return 0.85 def _calc_edc(self) -> float: """Full EDC for diagnostic reporting and Gini. PTH payments are 100% generative (EDC_housing_PTH = 0.0).""" if self.pth_resident: housing_edc = 0.00 else: monthly_rent = 1300 + np.random.uniform(-200, 500) housing_edc = monthly_rent / max(self.income, 1) consumer_debt_interest = np.random.uniform(50, 300) / max(self.income, 1) return min(housing_edc + consumer_debt_interest, 0.95) def _calc_edc_residual(self) -> float: """EDC_residual: consumer debt interest ONLY. Used in FBS to avoid double-counting housing costs in C_basic.""" consumer_debt_interest = np.random.uniform(50, 300) / max(self.income, 1) return min(consumer_debt_interest, 0.20) def _update_blei(self): """ BLEI = [L + γ·Y + BU_food_eff] / C_basic_cash BU_food_eff = food BU effective value only ($990/month per adult). Utility BU offset the $150 cash utility cost in C_basic → net = $0 for BLEI. γ transitions from 0.12 (entry) to 0.20 (CCO-established). L = cash_savings + h_AE·AE (AE excluded from W_accessible for CSI orthogonality). """ gamma = self._gamma() h_ae = self._ae_haircut() if self.pth_resident else 0.0 liquid = self.wealth + h_ae * self.acre_equity + (gamma * self.income) + self.bu_food_eff if self.cco_participant: daily_cash = (600 + 150 + 200) / 30 # PTH + util + transport; food covered by BU else: daily_cash = (1300 + 400 + 150 + 200) / 30 self.blei_score = liquid / max(daily_cash, 1) self.blei_tier = self._tier(self.blei_score) def _tier(self, blei: float) -> int: if blei < 7: return 0 # Crisis elif blei < 30: return 1 # Precarious elif blei < 120: return 2 # Threshold (BLEI poverty line) elif blei < 365: return 3 # Stable elif blei < 730: return 4 # Secure else: return 5 # Flourishing (> 730 days) def _update_fbs(self): """FBS uses EDC_residual (consumer debt only) — NOT full EDC. Housing cost is already in C_basic_cash_monthly.""" monthly_basic_cash = 950 if self.cco_participant else 1950 self.fbs = max(0.0, self.income + self.bu_monthly - monthly_basic_cash - self.edc_residual * self.income) def advancement_prob(self) -> float: """P(octave advance) gated by Financial Bandwidth Score. Mullainathan & Shafir (2013): scarcity taxes bandwidth; FBS=0 → P≈0.""" return 1.0 - np.exp(-self.lambda_cap * self.fbs) def update_wealth(self, cco_income: float, ptf_income: float, shock: float = 1.0, acre_rate: float = 0.04): """Monthly wealth update — extraction-accurate. r_a = 0.04 base (annual); monthly compounding as r_a/12. PTH payment routes to acre_equity (generative), not liquid wealth.""" total_income = (self.income + cco_income + ptf_income) * shock self.tenure_months += 1 if self.pth_resident: pth_pmt = 600 self.acre_equity += pth_pmt + self.acre_equity * (acre_rate / 12) housing_cash = 0 else: monthly_rent = 1300 + np.random.uniform(-100, 300) housing_cash = monthly_rent ptf_cost_savings = 400 if self.ptf_participant else 0 cash_essentials = (350 if self.cco_participant else 750) - ptf_cost_savings consumer_debt = self.edc_residual * self.income self.wealth += total_income - housing_cash - cash_essentials - consumer_debt self.wealth = max(self.wealth, 0.0) self._update_blei() self._update_fbs() @property def ptf_participant(self): return self.cco_participant
Simulation Engine

The Monte Carlo engine adds network-density-gated synergy, IPBI tracking for non-participating high-wealth agents, and BLEI-based welfare reporting across all 10,000 iterations. The ε cost pass-through model (BLEI paper §3.3) informs the calibrated range ε_food ∈ [1.50, 3.00]: lower bound reflects early-phase PTF operations; upper bound reflects full Mondragon-equivalent cooperative scale.

Sensitivity Analysis

156 parameter sensitivity tests across all key variables. The most important finding: BLEI outcomes remain above Tier 2 Threshold for CCO-PTF participants across all tested parameter combinations at Month 12, including the lowest ε_food (1.50×) and highest regional cost ($45/day) combinations. Month 0 values use γ = 0.12 (entry) and food BU effective value ($990).

Mathematical Framework

1. BLEI Temporal Stability Index

Primary welfare metric — hybrid stock/flow measure. BU food-BU effective value only in numerator (corrected accounting):
BLEI(i,t) = [ L(i,t) + γ(t)·Y(i,t) + BU_food_eff(i,t) ] / C_basic_cash(t) L(i,t) = liquid assets + h_AE · acre_equity (AE at tenure-dependent haircut) W_accessible = liquid_assets + γ·Y [excludes AE for CSI orthogonality] γ(t) = 0.12 (entry, Month 0–2) → transitions linearly to → 0.20 (Month 6+) Carroll (1997): guaranteed BU floor expands accessible income fraction BU_food_eff = 360 BU × $2.75 market equiv. = $990 per adult per month Utility BU (840 BU/month) offset the $150 cash utility cost in C_basic → net utility-BU BLEI contribution = $0 (avoids double-counting) EPPM uses full BU·ε_blended = $2,502 for total purchasing power measure C_basic_cash = $31.67/day (CCO-PTF) vs. $68.33/day (baseline) CCO: ($600 PTH + $150 util + $200 transport)/30 = $31.67/day AE liquidity haircut (h_AE) by tenure — t in months: Month 0–6: 0.15 (settlement lags; exit fees) Month 7–24: 0.40 (partial secondary market) Month 25–60: 0.65 (established equity; base-case projection cohort) Month 60+: 0.85 (mature; well-defined market)

2. Extraction-Corrected Dual Wealth Accumulation

W_total(t) = W_accessible(t) + AE(t) + Synergy(t) AE(t) = Σ [ PTH_payment · (1 + r_a/12)^(t−k) ] for k = 0..t r_a = 0.04 annual base; monthly compounding as r_a/12 per period (PTH residents only; AE excluded from W_accessible — CSI orthogonality) W_accessible(t) = W_accessible(0) · (1 + r_wealth/12)^t · (1 − EDC) r_wealth excludes all AE-linked returns (partition is strict) Synergy(t) = θ(ρ) · W_accessible · AE θ = 0 below 55% PTF density; scales to 0.25 at 90%+

3. FBS-Gated Advancement Probability

P(advance)(i) = 1 − exp( −λ(i) · FBS(i) ) FBS(i) = max( 0, Y + BU_monthly − C_basic_cash_monthly − EDC_residual · Y ) BU_monthly = $1,200 face value (cash-equivalent reduction in food expenditure) EDC_residual = consumer debt interest only (NOT housing — already in C_basic) Prevents double-subtraction of housing costs. λ(i) ~ Uniform(0.001, 0.008) Near-poverty CCO: FBS = $2,281/month → P(advance) meaningful Near-poverty base: FBS = $0/month → P(advance) ≈ 0

4. EDC-Adjusted Gini

W_net(i) = W_nominal(i) − EDC_full(i) · Y(i) · 12 Gini computed on W_net distribution across population. Nominal Gini retained as secondary reference metric only. Baseline EDC-adjusted Gini: 0.44–0.46 CCO-PTF EDC-adjusted Gini: 0.22 (full deployment, optimal parameters) Nordic reference benchmark: 0.27–0.29

5. Effective Purchasing Power Multiplier

EPPM(i) = [ Y + BU·ε_blended − EDC_CCO·Y ] / [ Y · (1 − EDC_baseline) ] BU·ε_blended: 360 BU × $2.75 + 840 BU × $1.80 = $990 + $1,512 = $2,502/adult/month (full food + utility BU value; used for total purchasing power measure only) BLEI uses $990 (food BU only) — see corrected accounting convention above. At poverty line (Y = $2,083): EPPM = ($2,083 + $2,502 − $52) / ($2,083 × 0.375) = $4,533 / $781 = 5.80×

6. IPBI Channel Notation

IPBI = α·CR + β·PH + π·LP + δ·PV + ζ·ME Channel weight π (labor productivity LP) is distinct from the BLEI liquidity coefficient γ. Full symbol table in BLEI Foundation Paper Appendix A. Conservative total: $22,800/yr (preferred; treats CR and PV as partially overlapping) Upper bound: $59,900/yr (assumes full channel independence; likely overstates 10–20%)
