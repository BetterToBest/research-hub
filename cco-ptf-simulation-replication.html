SYSTEM

Compassionism

The overarching framework developed by Duke Johnson, integrating five coordinated economic and governance architectures into a single system designed to structurally eliminate poverty while preserving market incentives and democratic governance.

CCO

Creative Currency Octaves

A dual-currency economic layer. Citizens opt in to receive Basic Units (BU) — a monthly allocation (~$1,200/adult) redeemable at PTF businesses for essential goods. Businesses that accept BU convert them to primary currency at merit-scaled rates (1×–9×), rewarding quality and community contribution.

BU

Basic Units

The community currency issued by the CCO system. Each BU is pegged 1:1 to the primary currency but redeemable at PTF establishments at below-market prices — generating a purchasing power premium (ε) averaging 2.64× at PTF food prices. BU expire monthly, ensuring they circulate toward genuine need rather than accumulating as savings.

PTF

Public Trust Foundations

Community-owned, democratically governed businesses — grocers, counter-service restaurants, utilities, childcare — that accept Basic Units and operate on a not-for-profit model. By eliminating shareholder profit extraction, PTF establishments achieve 40–60% overhead reductions (analogous to Mondragon's cooperative model), enabling the below-market pricing that generates the BU purchasing power premium.

PTH

Public Trust Housing

Community-owned residential housing in which monthly payments ($600/month at base case) accrue entirely to the resident's Acre Equity account rather than being lost to market rent or mortgage interest. This transforms a traditionally extractive expense into a wealth-building mechanism, analogous to how Community Land Trusts operate in practice across 46 US states.

AE

Acre Equity

The cumulative community equity built by PTH residents through their monthly payments, appreciating at approximately 4% per year. Unlike conventional home equity, Acre Equity is accessible at exit with a liquidity haircut that decreases as tenure increases — from ~10–20% in the first six months to ~80–90% at five or more years.

SZH

Social Zone Harmonization

The spatial governance layer that organizes the geographic deployment of PTF businesses and PTH communities within designated zones. SZH governs network density thresholds — the minimum PTF merchant participation needed to activate cooperative synergy effects (θ), which require at least 55% zone participation before activating.

CIP

Citizens Internet Portal

A privacy-first digital governance platform that administers CCO currency operations — conversion, distribution, merit assessment — and manages democratic voting for PTH and PTF decisions. CIP provides the transparent institutional layer that prevents elite capture and validates the system's democratic legitimacy.

BLEI

Basic Living Economic Index

The primary welfare metric in this framework. BLEI expresses how many days of basic living a household's accessible resources cover — a temporal stability measure that captures what income level and Gini coefficients cannot: whether a household has enough runway to take risks, invest in education, or start a business without needing immediate income.

EDC

Extractive Drain Coefficient

The fraction of gross income captured by financial contracts that build zero household wealth — rent, mortgage interest, consumer debt interest, predatory fees. The EDC is the measurement that reveals why two households earning $2,083/month can experience radically different economic trajectories: one paying extractive rent (EDC ~0.625); one building Acre Equity (EDC ~0.025).

FBS

Financial Bandwidth Score

Monthly residual resources above the basic living threshold — the "slack" that allows households to invest in education, creative work, or business formation. Grounded in Mullainathan & Shafir's (2013) scarcity cognition research: without positive FBS, cognitive bandwidth is consumed by immediate survival, making advancement structurally impossible regardless of individual capability.

EPPM

Effective Purchasing Power Multiplier

A dimensionless ratio expressing effective purchasing power of a CCO-PTF household relative to a baseline household at the same nominal income. An EPPM of 5.80× at the poverty line means a CCO-PTF participant has nearly six times the real purchasing capacity of an equivalent-income baseline household — primarily through BU purchasing power and EDC reduction.

CSI

Compound Stability Index

A forward-looking projection of BLEI trajectory over time, compounding initial stability by net wealth growth rate adjusted for extractive drain, plus accumulated Acre Equity. Under CCO-PTF, the CSI diverges exponentially from the baseline because generative contracts compound while extractive contracts stagnate — the core asymmetry standard metrics cannot see.

IPBI

Indirect Participant Benefit Index

Estimated annual welfare gains accruing to high-wealth households who do not directly participate in CCO-PTF but benefit from systemic poverty elimination — through crime reduction, lower healthcare premiums, property appreciation, and expanded consumer markets. Estimated at $22,800–$59,900 per year per high-wealth household (zero direct participation required).

These terms are formally defined and mathematically specified in the BLEI Foundation Paper. The full Compassionism system architecture — including CCO, PTF, PTH, SZH, and CIP formal definitions — is documented in the Executive Summary & Research Index.
Methodology Overview

This replication framework operationalizes the BLEI welfare measurement suite as a computational agent-based simulation. It is a calibrated institutional simulation architecture — not a conventional econometric study — designed to identify optimal system parameters and test framework behavior across thousands of parameter draws. Key parameters (ε, r_a, BU allocation) are grounded in three deployed real-world analogues: the Alaska Permanent Fund Dividend (BU allocation without dependency effects), Community Land Trusts across 46 US states (Acre Equity accumulation and r_a calibration), and Mondragon Corporation (cooperative overhead reduction underlying ε ≥ 2.0×).

Step 1 — BLEI-Initialized Agents

Agents initialized with BLEI, EDC, FBS, and Acre Equity state variables drawn from calibrated US wealth and housing distributions. PTH payments route to Acre Equity from initialization, at the appropriate liquidity haircut by tenure cohort.

Step 2 — Extraction-Accurate Dynamics

Monthly wealth updates distinguish extractive outflows (reduce BLEI, no AE) from generative payments (build AE, improve BLEI trajectory). PTH and market rent are modeled asymmetrically. FBS calculation uses EDC_residual (consumer debt only) to avoid double-counting housing costs already captured in C_basic.

Step 3 — FBS-Gated Advancement

Octave advancement probability is gated by Financial Bandwidth Score. P(advance) = 1 − exp(−λ × FBS). Agents at FBS = 0 cannot advance regardless of quality score. This reflects Mullainathan & Shafir's (2013) cognitive bandwidth research.

Step 4 — Network-Density Synergy

Synergy coefficient θ activates only above 55% PTF merchant participation within a SZH zone, scaling linearly to 0.25 at 90%+ density. Below this threshold, cooperative pricing synergies do not activate and ε reflects early-phase efficiency (1.5–2.0×) rather than mature cooperative scale (2.64×).

Step 5 — Full-Cohort Welfare

High-wealth non-participants tracked separately with IPBI benefit flows representing crime reduction, public health improvement, property value appreciation, and labor productivity gains. Conservative lower-bound IPBI ($22,800/yr) is the preferred metric; upper bound ($59,900/yr) assumes full channel independence.

Step 6 — EDC-Adjusted Gini

Gini reported on extraction-adjusted net wealth: W_net = W_nominal − (EDC × Y × 12). This corrects the standard Gini's blindness to the direction of financial flows: two households with identical nominal wealth but different EDC levels have different structural inequality positions. Nominal Gini retained as secondary reference only.

BLEI Temporal Stability Tier System

The six BLEI tiers replace binary poverty/non-poverty classification and gate agent advancement probability in the simulation. Tier assignment derives from BLEI score (days of basic living covered). The Flourishing tier threshold of 730 days — two years — reflects the capital commitment horizon required for entrepreneurial ventures to become viable without required break-even within the first year. Tier characterizations are grounded in Carroll's (1997) buffer-stock saving theory and Mullainathan & Shafir's (2013) cognitive bandwidth research.

Agent Architecture — Extraction-Accurate Model

The corrected agent class adds six state variables and revises the wealth update function to separate extractive and generative financial flows. The FBS calculation uses EDC_residual (consumer debt interest only) rather than the full EDC to avoid double-counting housing costs already captured in C_basic_cash_monthly. Key corrections from prior simulation code are annotated inline.

# ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════ # Agent — Extraction-Accurate BLEI-Integrated Model # Corrects: extraction symmetry, BU epsilon, octave-scaling, FBS gating # Key addition: AE liquidity haircut by tenure; EDC_residual in FBS # ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════ class Agent: def __init__(self, agent_id: int): self.id = agent_id self.tenure_months = 0 # months in PTH; governs AE liquidity haircut # ── Primary state ────────────────────────────────────────────── self.wealth = np.random.lognormal(10.5, 1.2) self.income = np.random.lognormal(3.5, 0.5) # monthly primary-currency income # ── Participation flags ──────────────────────────────────────── self.cco_participant = np.random.choice([True, False], p=[0.78, 0.22]) self.pth_resident = np.random.choice([True, False], p=[0.20, 0.80]) self.octave_level = 0 self.quality_score = np.random.uniform(1, 9) # ── BLEI state variables (corrected model) ───────────────────── # BU_MONTHLY is FLAT — not scaled by octave level. # Octave controls CONVERSION CAPACITY, not allocation amount. # Prior bug: bu_allocation = self.bu * octaveCap ← INCORRECT self.bu_monthly = 1200.0 if self.cco_participant else 0.0 self.bu_epsilon = 2.64 # BU effective purchasing power at PTF food prices (range 1.50–3.00) self.acre_equity = 0.0 # accumulated PTH equity (generative; enter BLEI at liquidity haircut) self.edc_full = self._calc_edc() # full EDC for diagnostic / Gini reporting self.edc_residual = self._calc_edc_residual() # consumer debt only — used in FBS (no double-count) self.fbs = 0.0 # Financial Bandwidth Score: above-subsistence residual self.blei_score = 0.0 # days of basic living covered — primary welfare metric self.blei_tier = 0 # 0=Crisis … 5=Flourishing self.lambda_cap = np.random.uniform(0.001, 0.008) # capability coefficient self._update_blei() self._update_fbs() def _ae_haircut(self) -> float: """ Acre Equity liquidity haircut by PTH tenure. Reflects settlement lags, exit fees, and secondary-market maturity. Calibrated against Community Land Trust exit data (Davis & Demetrowitz 2003). """ m = self.tenure_months if m < 7: return 0.15 # Month 0–6: minimal liquidity elif m < 25: return 0.40 # Month 7–24: partial secondary market elif m < 61: return 0.65 # Month 25–60: established equity else: return 0.85 # Month 60+: mature, well-defined market def _calc_edc(self) -> float: """ Full Extractive Drain Coefficient for diagnostic reporting and Gini. Prior bug: PTH treated identically to rent (symmetric extraction). Corrected: PTH payments are 100% generative (EDC_housing_PTH = 0.0). """ if self.pth_resident: housing_edc = 0.00 # PTH payments build Acre Equity — zero extraction else: monthly_rent = 1300 + np.random.uniform(-200, 500) housing_edc = monthly_rent / max(self.income, 1) consumer_debt_interest = np.random.uniform(50, 300) / max(self.income, 1) return min(housing_edc + consumer_debt_interest, 0.95) def _calc_edc_residual(self) -> float: """ EDC_residual: consumer debt interest ONLY. Used in FBS calculation to avoid double-counting housing costs already captured in C_basic_cash_monthly. """ consumer_debt_interest = np.random.uniform(50, 300) / max(self.income, 1) return min(consumer_debt_interest, 0.20) def _update_blei(self): """ BLEI = [L + γ·Y + BU·ε] / C_basic_cash L includes AE at liquidity haircut h_AE (tenure-dependent). C_basic_cash differs by system: food covered by BU under CCO reduces denominator. """ gamma = 0.20 if self.cco_participant else 0.12 bu_eff = self.bu_monthly * self.bu_epsilon if self.cco_participant else 0.0 h_ae = self._ae_haircut() if self.pth_resident else 0.0 liquid = self.wealth + h_ae * self.acre_equity + (gamma * self.income) + bu_eff if self.cco_participant: daily_cash = (600 + 150 + 200) / 30 # PTH + utilities + transport; food covered by BU else: daily_cash = (1300 + 400 + 150 + 200) / 30 # rent + food + utilities + transport self.blei_score = liquid / max(daily_cash, 1) self.blei_tier = self._tier(self.blei_score) def _tier(self, blei: float) -> int: if blei < 7: return 0 # Crisis elif blei < 30: return 1 # Precarious elif blei < 120: return 2 # Threshold (BLEI-defined poverty line) elif blei < 365: return 3 # Stable elif blei < 730: return 4 # Secure else: return 5 # Flourishing (> 730 days = 2-year entrepreneurial horizon) def _update_fbs(self): """ FBS uses EDC_residual (consumer debt only) — NOT full EDC. Housing cost is already in C_basic_cash_monthly; including it in EDC·Y as well would double-subtract the same expense. """ monthly_basic_cash = 950 if self.cco_participant else 1950 self.fbs = max(0.0, self.income + self.bu_monthly - monthly_basic_cash - self.edc_residual * self.income) def advancement_prob(self) -> float: """P(octave advance) gated by Financial Bandwidth Score. Prior bug: advancement probability not linked to FBS. Grounding: Mullainathan & Shafir (2013) — scarcity taxes cognitive bandwidth, making advancement structurally infeasible at FBS = 0. """ return 1.0 - np.exp(-self.lambda_cap * self.fbs) def update_wealth(self, cco_income: float, ptf_income: float, shock: float = 1.0, acre_rate: float = 0.04): """ Monthly wealth update — extraction-accurate. Prior bug: PTH payment subtracted from liquid wealth same as rent. Corrected: PTH payment routes to self.acre_equity (generative). r_a = 0.04 base; calibrated against CLT long-run appreciation data. """ total_income = (self.income + cco_income + ptf_income) * shock self.tenure_months += 1 if self.pth_resident: pth_pmt = 600 # PTH payment BUILDS Acre Equity — does NOT reduce liquid wealth self.acre_equity += pth_pmt + self.acre_equity * (acre_rate / 12) housing_cash = 0 else: monthly_rent = 1300 + np.random.uniform(-100, 300) housing_cash = monthly_rent # fully extractive; leaves wealth sphere permanently # PTF savings = cost savings vs. market price (price-level differential, not % of wage) # Prior bug: ptf_savings = wage * ptf_savings_pct ← should be price-level difference ptf_cost_savings = 400 if self.ptf_participant else 0 cash_essentials = (350 if self.cco_participant else 750) - ptf_cost_savings consumer_debt = self.edc_residual * self.income # residual only; housing not double-counted self.wealth += total_income - housing_cash - cash_essentials - consumer_debt self.wealth = max(self.wealth, 0.0) self._update_blei() self._update_fbs() @property def ptf_participant(self): return self.cco_participant
Simulation Engine

The Monte Carlo engine adds network-density-gated synergy, IPBI tracking for non-participating high-wealth agents, and BLEI-based welfare reporting across all 10,000 iterations. Parameters are drawn from ranges calibrated against real-world analogues: ε from Mondragon food cooperative data, acre_rate from CLT appreciation literature, bu_monthly from APF-scale analysis.

Returns 0.0 below the network activation threshold. Below threshold, cooperative pricing synergies do not activate and ε reflects early-phase efficiency rather than mature scale.""" if density < NETWORK_THRESHOLD: return 0.0 t = min((density - NETWORK_THRESHOLD) / 0.35, 1.0) return SYNERGY_THETA_MIN + t * (SYNERGY_THETA_MAX - SYNERGY_THETA_MIN) class MonteCarloAnalysis: def __init__(self, n=SIMULATION_RUNS): self.n = n def run(self): results = [] for _ in range(self.n): params = { 'bu_monthly': np.random.uniform(1000, 1500), 'pth_uptake': np.random.uniform(0.15, 0.25), # PTH uptake: 15–25%; distinct from PTF share 'ptf_share': np.random.uniform(0.12, 0.25), # PTF market share: 12–25%; distinct from PTH uptake 'network_density': np.random.uniform(0.40, 0.95), 'participation': np.random.uniform(0.60, 0.95), 'bu_epsilon': np.random.uniform(1.50, 3.00), # Mondragon calibrated range 'acre_rate': np.random.uniform(0.02, 0.07), # CLT literature range 'octave_max': np.random.randint(4, 9), 'quality_accuracy':np.random.uniform(0.70, 0.95), } theta = synergy_theta(params['network_density']) results.append(self._run_scenario(params, theta)) return self._aggregate(results) def validate(self, r: dict): """BLEI-based internal calibration benchmarks. These confirm the simulation reaches its design targets — they are calibration checks, not external empirical validation.""" assert r['blei_threshold_rate'][1] > 0.95, "BLEI Threshold rate must exceed 95%" assert r['blei_secure_rate'][1] > 0.80, "BLEI Secure rate must exceed 80%" assert r['gini_edc_adjusted'][1] < 0.22, "EDC-adjusted Gini must be < 0.22" assert r['median_eppm'][1] > 2.00, "Median EPPM must exceed 2.0×" assert r['system_stability'] > 0.90, "System stability must exceed 90%" print("All internal calibration benchmarks passed.")

Mathematical Framework

1. BLEI Temporal Stability Index

Primary welfare metric — hybrid stock/flow measure of temporal stability:
BLEI(i,t) = [ L(i,t) + γ·Y(i,t) + BU_monthly · ε ] / C_basic_cash(t) L(i,t) = liquid assets + h_AE · acre_equity (AE at tenure-dependent haircut) γ = 0.20 (CCO), 0.12 (baseline) (Carroll 1997 buffer-stock grounding) BU_monthly · ε = $1,200 × 2.64 = $3,168 food-equivalent per adult per month C_basic_cash = $31.67/day (CCO-PTF) vs. $68.33/day (baseline) AE liquidity haircut (h_AE) by tenure: Month 0–6: 0.15 (settlement lags; exit fees; minimal secondary market) Month 7–24: 0.40 (partial secondary market access) Month 25–60: 0.65 (established equity; base-case projection cohort) Month 60+: 0.85 (mature; well-defined market)

2. Extraction-Corrected Dual Wealth Accumulation

W_total(t) = W_liquid(t) + AE(t) + Synergy(t) AE(t) = Σ [ PTH_payment · (1 + r_a)^(k/12) ] for k = 0..t (PTH residents only; zero for market renters) Calibration: r_a = 0.04 base, range 0.02–0.07 (CLT literature) Synergy(t) = θ(ρ) · W_liquid · AE where θ(ρ) is network-density-gated (ρ = PTF zone participation rate) θ = 0 below 55%; scales to 0.25 at 90%+

3. FBS-Gated Advancement Probability

P(advance)(i) = 1 − exp( −λ(i) · FBS(i) ) FBS(i) = max( 0, Y + BU_monthly − C_basic_cash_monthly − EDC_residual · Y ) EDC_residual = consumer debt interest only (NOT housing — already in C_basic) This separation prevents double-subtraction of housing costs. λ(i) ~ Uniform(0.001, 0.008) [individual capability coefficient] Near-poverty CCO: FBS = $2,281/month → P(advance) meaningful Near-poverty base: FBS = $0/month → P(advance) ≈ 0 Behavioral grounding: Mullainathan & Shafir (2013) — cognitive bandwidth research demonstrates FBS = 0 structurally prevents advancement.

4. EDC-Adjusted Gini

W_net(i) = W_nominal(i) − EDC_full(i) · Y(i) · 12 Gini computed on W_net distribution across population. Nominal Gini reported as secondary reference metric only. Rationale: Two households with identical W_nominal but EDC = 0.025 vs. EDC = 0.625 face structurally different wealth trajectories. The EDC-adjusted measure captures the extractive burden that nominal Gini ignores. Baseline EDC-adjusted Gini: 0.44–0.46 (extraction raises effective inequality above nominal) CCO-PTF EDC-adjusted Gini: 0.22 (full deployment, optimal parameters) Nordic reference benchmark: 0.27–0.29 (best sustained outcome under existing systems)

5. Effective Purchasing Power Multiplier

EPPM(i) = [ Y + (BU · ε_blended) − EDC_CCO · Y ] / [ Y · (1 − EDC_baseline) ] BU · ε_blended: 360 BU × $2.75 + 840 BU × $1.80 = $990 + $1,512 = $2,502/adult/month Unit: dimensionless ratio (EPPM = 1.00 at baseline; > 1.00 indicates CCO-PTF improvement) At poverty line (Y = $2,083): EPPM = ($2,083 + $2,502 − $52) / ($2,083 × 0.375) = $4,533 / $781 = 5.80× Median household (Y = $6,215, 2 adults): EPPM = ($6,215 + $5,004 − $186) / ($6,215 × 0.636) = $11,033 / $3,953 = 2.79×
